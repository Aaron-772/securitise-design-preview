PART II — THE STANDARD

Five articles. Held for every client.

01

Security precedes everything.

It is not a product we sell you. Every environment is built on the CIS controls — the framework designed to stop real-world attacks — before anything else is done.

In practice

What does a security-first MSP actually do differently?

A security-first MSP builds your IT on a recognised security framework before taking on day-to-day support. Securitise implements the CIS Controls — the prioritised safeguards used worldwide to stop ransomware, phishing and account takeover — as the baseline for every UK business we manage.

  • CIS Controls implemented as standard, not quoted as extras
  • Microsoft 365, devices and backups hardened before go-live
  • One certified configuration standard across every client
Foundation
02

Certification is included, not sold.

Every client is taken to Cyber Essentials Plus — the UK government-backed, independently assessed certification that insurers and contracts increasingly demand.

In practice

Why does Cyber Essentials Plus matter for a UK SME?

Cyber Essentials Plus is the UK government-backed cyber security certification in which an independent assessor tests your defences. It is increasingly required to bid for public-sector contracts, expected in enterprise supply chains, and asked about on cyber insurance questionnaires. Securitise includes CE+ certification in onboarding for every client — within [90] days.

  • Bid-ready for contracts that require Cyber Essentials
  • Insurer questionnaires answered with evidence, not hope
  • Re-certified every year as part of the service
Certified
03

No client speaks to a script.

There is no first line here. Every matter is handled by a second-line engineer or above — someone with the judgement and the authority to resolve it, first time.

In practice

How is this different from a normal IT helpdesk?

Traditional IT support puts a script-reading first line between you and the people who can fix things — that is why tickets bounce for days. Securitise deleted that tier. Every call and ticket is answered by a second-line engineer or above, with a maximum first-response time of [X] minutes committed in writing.

  • No call queues, no triage scripts, no escalation theatre
  • A named engineer who knows your business and your history
  • Response times in the contract, not the brochure
Senior only
04

The work is shown, not implied.

Monitoring runs day and night, and what it catches is written up every Friday in plain English. You will never have to wonder what you are paying for.

In practice

What do I actually see for my money?

Every Securitise client receives the Friday Report: a plain-English weekly account of the phishing emails blocked, the vulnerabilities patched, the backups tested and what is planned next. It is readable in three minutes and written for a business owner, not an engineer — proactive 24/7 monitoring, made visible.

  • Weekly written evidence of threats stopped and work done
  • Backup restores tested and timed — recovery proven, not assumed
  • Forward notice of costs: renewals and replacements flagged early
Evidenced
05

Few clients, by design.

A deliberately small client list is what makes the first four articles possible. We would rather decline good business than dilute the standard of care.

In practice

Who is Securitise right for?

Securitise is a boutique, founder-led IT practice for UK businesses of roughly 20–150 people that want security and IT handled to one certified standard by people they can name. We take on a limited number of new clients each quarter, and the discovery call assesses fit on both sides — honestly.

  • UK SMEs that treat their operations seriously
  • A named senior engineer, not a rotating queue
  • Not for price-led or break-fix buyers — we say so up front
